What IS a 990?

A nonprofit's annual tax return, filed with the IRS.

  • How nonprofits prove they're operating within the rules of their tax-exempt status
  • Unlike your personal taxes — it's completely public. Anyone can Google your client's 990 right now.
  • It tells the story of where money came from and where it went — programs, admin, fundraising
  • Miss 3 years in a row? The IRS revokes the nonprofit status automatically.

💡 ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer — free, public, anyone can look up any 990 for free. Donors and grantmakers use this to evaluate orgs.

It's a public document

Every dollar on this form — revenue, salaries, grants — is visible to anyone with a browser. Donors, grantmakers, journalists, competitors.

It comes from your books

Every number here is built from QBO. The 990 is only as accurate as the year-end close your accounting team delivers.

This is 12 pages + schedules

The full 990 can run 50–100+ pages once all schedules are attached. The EZ is 4 pages. The Postcard is 5 questions.

The Fine Print 🔍

The $50K postcard threshold is a 3-year average, not a single-year number.

Example:

Year 1: $100K  ·  Year 2: $40K

Average = $70K → They file the 990-EZ. The little guy just grew up.

  • Once you cross a threshold consistently, you stay in that tier
  • If an org shrinks back, they can downgrade — but it takes time
  • This is exactly why accurate year-end numbers matter so much — they determine next year's form

Audit vs. Review vs. Nothing

Determined by state law and how much money the org raised.

🔬

Audit

Full independent examination. CPA tests transactions, issues a formal opinion. Required by certain states above revenue thresholds.

🔍

Review

Less intensive. CPA asks questions, runs analytics, issues limited assurance. Real work — just not full testing of every transaction.

🎉

Nothing

Lucky them! Smaller orgs or certain states don't require an independent financial review. File the 990 and done.

📋 Every state is different. We built a master guide: "Taxes — Audit Requirements by State (2025)"

Your Role: Document Gatherers 📁

What CPAs Typically Request

  • Bank & investment statements (year-end)
  • Payroll records — wages by employee & role
  • Invoices, vendor contracts, large receipts
  • EINs, org documents, prior year 990
  • Grant award letters & restricted fund details
  • Board meeting minutes (yes, really)

How to Submit

  • CPA client portal — each audit/review has its own
  • Karbon task — mark it done when you upload
  • When in doubt: ask Natalie before the deadline

⚡ CPAs bill by the hour. Slow responses = expensive delays = unhappy clients.

Your Role: Year-End Close Team 📊

Clean books = smooth tax season. That's the whole thing.

Accounting Team — The Foundation

  • All transactions coded with the right category & class
  • Intercompany transactions fully reconciled
  • Bank reconciliations complete across all accounts
  • Year-end close marked done in Karbon

Why It Directly Impacts the 990

  • CPAs build the 990 line by line from your QBO numbers
  • Functional expense split comes entirely from how transactions are coded
  • One miscoded transaction can delay an entire filing
  • Messy books = expensive back-and-forth = late 990
